## Crafting the perfect title

Your title is the star of the show. Here's how to make it memorable:

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Keep it concise">
    Shorter titles have more impact and are easier to read on screen. Aim for 1-4 words.

    **Good examples:**
    - "Sarah Miller"
    - "The Dark Lord"
    - "Phoenix Rising"

    **Too long:**
    - "The Adventures of Sarah Miller and Her Magical Friends"
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Use title case">
    Capitalize the first letter of each major word for a professional, polished look.

    **Recommended:**
    - "The Half-Blood Prince"
    - "Order of the Phoenix"

    **Avoid:**
    - "the half-blood prince"
    - "ORDER OF THE PHOENIX"
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Consider your audience">
    Think about who will see this video and what impression you want to make.

    - **For personal use:** Your name or nickname
    - **For projects:** A descriptive project title
    - **For gifts:** The recipient's name or a meaningful phrase
    - **For content:** Something that matches your brand
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Writing compelling subtitles

The subtitle adds personality and context to your video:

<Tip>
  Use your subtitle to tell a story or add character details that complement your title.
</Tip>

### Subtitle strategies

**Match the Harry Potter universe:**
- Reference Hogwarts houses ("Pride of Slytherin")
- Mention magical abilities ("Master of Defensive Charms")
- Use magical positions ("Seeker and Captain")
- Include wand details ("Oak and Phoenix Feather")

**Make it personal:**
- Your profession with a magical twist ("Muggle-Born Developer")
- A personal motto or catchphrase
- Your achievements or goals
- A meaningful date or milestone

**Create atmosphere:**
- Use evocative language ("Guardian of Secrets")
- Set a tone ("Born from Fire and Ash")
- Build intrigue ("The Prophecy Unfolds")

<Warning>
  Avoid subtitles that are longer than your title. This can make the composition look unbalanced.
</Warning>

## Email best practices

<Info>
  Your video will be delivered via email within a few hours of submission.
</Info>

**To ensure smooth delivery:**

1. **Use a reliable email provider** - Gmail, Outlook, or other major providers work best
2. **Check spam/junk folders** - Sometimes delivery emails get filtered
3. **Whitelist the sender** - Add the sending domain to your contacts
4. **Ensure sufficient storage** - Make sure your inbox isn't full
5. **Use a professional email** - Avoid temporary or disposable email addresses
